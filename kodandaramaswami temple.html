Kadapa Kodanda Ramaswami Temple


                 

Description

The Kodanda Ramaswami Temple, dedicated to Lord Rama, is a significant temple located in Kadapa district. This temple is renowned for its exquisite architecture and is a major pilgrimage site for devotees of Lord Rama.

Built:

The temple is believed to have been built in the 18th century, showcasing the rich architectural heritage of the region.

Deity:

The temple features a stunning idol of Lord Rama along with Sita and Lakshmana, beautifully adorned during festivals.

Video:

Architectural Features

Style:

The temple exhibits Dravidian architectural style, characterized by intricate carvings and sculptures that depict various scenes from the Ramayana.

Main Shrine:

The main shrine houses the idols of Lord Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana, which are beautifully crafted and a sight to behold.

Intricate Carvings:

The temple walls are adorned with exquisite carvings that narrate stories from the Ramayana, showcasing the craftsmanship of ancient artisans.

Visitor Information

Guides:

Local guides are available to provide insights into the temple's history and architectural significance.

Timings:

The temple is open from 6:00 AM to 9:00 PM daily.

Entry Fee:

Free

Significant Features:

Description: A prominent statue of Nandi, the sacred bull, is located near the entrance, symbolizing strength and devotion.

Tips for Visitors

Comfortable Clothing:

Wear modest clothing that covers shoulders and knees to show respect in the temple.

Respect:

Maintain silence and show respect to the rituals and traditions observed in the temple premises.
