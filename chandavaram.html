Chandravaram Buddhist Site


                 

Description:

Chandravaram is located in the serene landscapes of Andhra Pradesh, surrounded by hills and lush greenery, making it an ideal spot for peace seekers and history buffs.

Video

History:

The Chandravaram site dates back to the 3rd century BCE and is significant for its ancient Buddhist rock-cut caves and stupas, providing a glimpse into the region's Buddhist heritage.

Architecture

The site features remarkable rock-cut architecture, with intricately carved caves and stupas that showcase the artistic skills of ancient craftsmen.

Culture and Heritage

Chandravaram is a significant cultural site, often hosting events and festivals that celebrate its rich Buddhist heritage and traditions.

Tourism

This site attracts tourists for its historical significance, rock-cut caves, and serene environment. It's a perfect place for exploring history and spirituality.

Accessibility

Chandravaram is accessible by road, with local transport options available, making it easy for visitors to reach from nearby towns and cities.
