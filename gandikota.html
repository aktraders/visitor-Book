Gandikota Fort


                 

Description

Gandikota Fort, often referred to as the "Grand Canyon of India," is located in the Kadapa district of Andhra Pradesh. It was built in the 14th century and boasts stunning views of the Pennar River, along with impressive architecture that reflects the rich history of the region.

Video

Architecture

Fortification:

The fort features massive walls and bastions, built to withstand invasions. Its architecture exhibits a mix of Hindu and Islamic styles, showcasing the cultural diversity of the region.

Structures:

Notable structures within the fort include:

  • Main Gate: The grand entrance that leads to the fort complex.
  • Palace Ruins: The remnants of royal residences highlight the fort's historical significance.
  • Temples: Ancient temples dedicated to various deities can be explored within the fort.

Best Time to Visit

Season:

The ideal time to visit Gandikota Fort is between November and March, when the weather is cooler and more suitable for outdoor exploration.

Visitor Information

Accessibility:

The fort is easily reachable by road from Kadapa, which is well-connected to major cities. Public and private transportation options are available.

Timings:

The fort is generally open for visitors from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM. It's best to check for any updates before your visit.

Entry Fee:

There is usually a nominal entry fee, which may vary. Some specific areas within the fort might have additional charges.
