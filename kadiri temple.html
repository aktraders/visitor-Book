Kadiri Temple


                 

Description

Kadiri Temple, dedicated to Lord Lakshmi Narasimha, is located in Kadiri, Andhra Pradesh. This ancient temple is known for its architectural beauty and spiritual significance, attracting many devotees and tourists.

Built:

The temple dates back to the 12th century, showcasing exquisite architecture from the period.

Deity:

The main deity is Lord Lakshmi Narasimha, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, worshipped here in the form of a stunning idol.

Video:

Architectural Features

Style:

The temple features intricate carvings and sculptures that reflect the rich Dravidian architectural style.

Main Shrine:

The main shrine enshrines the idol of Lord Lakshmi Narasimha, which is an object of devotion for many pilgrims.

Intricate Carvings:

Visitors can admire the elaborate carvings on the temple walls, depicting various deities and mythological tales.

Visitor Information

Guides:

Local guides are available to offer insights into the temple's history and significance.

Timings:

The temple is open from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily.

Entry Fee:

Free

Significant Features:

A statue of Nandi, the sacred bull, is prominently placed near the temple entrance, symbolizing devotion and strength.

Tips for Visitors

Comfortable Clothing:

Wear modest clothing that covers shoulders and knees to show respect in the temple.

Respect:

Maintain silence and respect the rituals and traditions observed within the temple premises.
