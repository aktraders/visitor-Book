Araku

Araku Valley is about 115 Km. from Visakhapatnam lying on the top of the eastern ghats. The area of the valley is roughly 36 Sq.Kms. and the altitude is between 600 and 900 meters above mean sea level. The entire area is inhabited by aboriginal tribes and attracts visitors from all over the country. The journey to Araku Valley on the ghat road with thick forests on either side is itself interesting and thrilling.

Attraction

Nature and Scenic Beauty:

Valley Views: The valley is known for its lush green landscapes, picturesque hills, and beautiful valleys.

Waterfalls: Key waterfalls include the Katiki Waterfalls and Chaparai Waterfalls.

video

Tribal Culture:

Tribal Heritage: The region is home to various tribal communities, including the Gadabas, Kondhas, and others. Visitors can explore their traditional lifestyles and handicrafts.

Coffee Plantations:

Coffee Estates: Araku Valley is famous for its coffee plantations. The Coffee Museum here provides insights into the coffee-growing process.

Adventure and Trekking:

Trekking Trails: There are several trekking routes in and around Araku Valley, offering opportunities for adventure and exploration.

Places of Interest:

Borra Caves: These limestone caves, located about 30 kilometers from Araku Valley, are one of the major attractions. They are renowned for their impressive stalactites and stalagmites formations.

Ananthagiri Hills: These hills are known for their scenic beauty and are part of the Araku Valley region.

Accessibility

By Road: Araku Valley is accessible by road from Visakhapatnam. The drive is scenic and takes around 3-4 hours.

By Train: The Araku Railway Station is well-connected by train. The journey by train offers stunning views of the Eastern Ghats and is a popular choice for travelers.

By Air: The nearest airport is in Visakhapatnam, from where you can take a cab or bus to Araku Valley.

Best time to visit:

Winter (November to February): This is the best time to visit due to the pleasant weather. Monsoon (June to September): The region receives moderate to heavy rainfall, making the landscape lush and green, but travel might be affected by rain.

Tips for Travelers

Travel Light: Bring light clothing, but also a few warm layers for the cooler evenings.

Health Precautions: Carry necessary medications, especially if you are prone to motion sickness, as the drive involves winding roads.

Respect Local Culture: Be mindful and respectful of the local tribal communities and their traditions.
