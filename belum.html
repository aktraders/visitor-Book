Belum Caves


                 

Description

Belum Caves are the largest caves in India, located in the Kurnool district of Andhra Pradesh. They are famous for their stalactites and stalagmites, with impressive formations and extensive passages that make them a remarkable geological site.

Video

Cave Structure:

Main Cave System:

The caves feature a complex system of tunnels and chambers, with some sections being as deep as 1500 meters. The formations are estimated to be over 1.5 million years old.

How to Reach:

By Air:

The nearest airport is Rajahmundry Airport (about 180 km away). From there, you can take a taxi or bus.

By Train:

The nearest major railway station is Kurnool. You can hire a taxi or take a local bus from the station to Belum.

By Road:

Well-connected by road with regular bus services and taxis from nearby cities like Kurnool and Hyderabad.

Local Attractions:

Kurnool:

A city known for its rich history and places of interest like Konda Reddy Fort.

Cave:

Explore other geological formations in the region that add to the natural beauty of Andhra Pradesh.

Tips for Visitors

Dress Code:

Wear comfortable clothing suitable for exploring caves. Sturdy footwear is recommended.

Footwear:

Be prepared to remove footwear before entering certain sections of the caves.

Offerings:

While not required, you may bring offerings such as flowers. Ensure that any offerings align with local customs and practices.
