Salihundam


                 

Description

Salihundam is an ancient Buddhist site located in the Srikakulam district of Andhra Pradesh. It features a collection of stupas and rock-cut sculptures, reflecting the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Historical Significance:

Dating back to the 3rd century BCE, Salihundam was an important center for Buddhism, showcasing ancient monastic architecture and artistic expressions.

Architectural Features

Stupas:

The site contains several stupas that are intricately designed and serve as significant relics of Buddhist architecture.

Sculptures:

Visitors can admire beautifully carved sculptures depicting various aspects of Buddhist teachings and figures.

Visitor Information

Guides:

Local guides are available to provide insights into the history and significance of the site.

Timings:

The site is open from 6:00 AM to 7:00 PM daily.

Entry Fee:

Free

Tips for Visitors

Comfortable Footwear:

Wear comfortable shoes as the site involves walking and exploring the surroundings.

Respect:

Maintain a respectful attitude while exploring the historical site and adhere to any guidelines provided by the management.
