Shiridi sai baba temple


                 

Description

The Shirdi Sai Baba Temple in Puttaparthi, also known as the Prasanthi Nilayam Sai Baba Temple, is a significant pilgrimage site dedicated to Sai Baba of Shirdi. Here’s a comprehensive guide for visiting this important temple:

video

Historical Significance

Sathya Sai Baba:

He was a prominent spiritual leader and philanthropist who claimed to be the reincarnation of Sai Baba of Shirdi. Puttaparthi became the center of his activities, and he established the Prasanthi Nilayam ashram as a spiritual and educational institution.

Prasanthi Nilayam:

The name means “Abode of Peace,” and it serves as the central place for the followers of Sathya Sai Baba.

Best Time to Visit

Season:

The best time to visit Chandragiri Fort is between November and March when the weather is cooler and more pleasant.

Visitor Information

Daily Darshan:

Typically open from 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 4:00 PM to 6:00 PM.

Special Events:

Timings may vary during special events and festivals.

Entry Fee:

Entry to the temple and ashram is generally free, but donations are welcomed.

How to Reach

by air

Nearest Airport: Banglore Kempegowda International Airport (BLR) Distance: Approximately 120 kilometers from Puttaparthi. Transport: You can hire a taxi or take a bus from the airport to Puttaparthi.

by train

Nearest Railway Station: Puttaparthi Railway Station Distance: About 3 kilometers from the temple.

by road

Local Transport: Autos, taxis, and buses are available for travel within Puttaparthi and to the temple.

Rules and Guidelines

Dress Code

Modest attire is required. Avoid wearing revealing clothing.

Carry Water and Snacks:

Outside food and drinks are typically not allowed inside the ashram. Free meals are usually provided in the ashram’s dining facilities.

Photography:

Photography is generally prohibited inside the temple and ashram areas.
